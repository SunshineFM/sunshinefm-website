# SunshineFM — Friday 01-16-2026

HEADLINE: The AI Infrastructure War Just Landed 15 Minutes From Coachella. Are We Ready?
---

A $10 billion AI data center is being proposed in Imperial County. That’s 15 minutes from Coachella, and it's set to become the largest data center project in California history. Most residents still don’t know about it. That’s the first problem. The facility itself? A staggering 950,000 square feet, drawing 330 megawatts of power, backed by 220 Tesla megapacks and 132 natural gas generators. This isn’t hypothetical. This is happening.

Developers offer the glossy pitch: transformative investment, local jobs, $9 billion added to the county’s assessed value. They claim "water positive" – using reclaimed wastewater – and promise "interruptible service" to protect the grid. These are exactly the promises you'd expect. But here’s the reality: the Imperial Irrigation District (IID) itself has stated it “don’t have the capability to reliably support this load with existing resources” and "cannot commit to serve the requested load." Translation: the grid can’t handle it. So, who pays for the inevitable upgrades? Who pays when power gets cut? You do. And the water? A mid-sized data center can consume 300,000 gallons per day. In a region already battling Colorado River limits, that number should be terrifying. This project, by the way, sidestepped California environmental review, and the City of Imperial had to sue to stop it. This isn’t smart development. It’s a land grab by the quiet approval method.

This isn’t just a local fight. Governors in Pennsylvania, Ohio, and Virginia are holding emergency meetings right now over AI data centers driving up electricity costs for residents. Microsoft has committed to subsidizing residential rates in those areas – not because the power supply is abundant, but because base rates *will* rise. It's magical PR for a fundamental problem. Here’s the kicker for our valley: IID has received *multiple* data center inquiries, some ranging up to a staggering 1 gigawatt per site. This isn't one rogue project. This is a wave. The "frontier model wars" – who built the smartest AI – are largely over. The real battle now is for power, water, and land to run them. The infrastructure wars have begun.

For founders and anyone building a "second chapter" in the Coachella Valley in 2026, this isn't abstract. It's the ground truth. Your innovative app, your AI-powered service – it all runs on this kind of infrastructure. The question for Palm Springs, Coachella, and Rancho Mirage isn’t whether these data centers are coming. It’s whether you’ll have a voice in how it happens. Because if we wait until county approval, we're too late. We need our city councils asking direct questions, *now*: Who are you talking to? What’s the real plan for power and water? Who pays for grid upgrades? And will residents be informed before, or after, the decision? For an SF founder weighing a move to the desert, understanding this transparency (or lack thereof) is far more important than the latest VC round. We can either be proactive participants or passive recipients. The choice defines our future.
