## Amazon Gets Careful (And That's Good News)

Amazon just dropped AI health agents into One Medical, and honestly, the most interesting part isn't what it does—it's how carefully they're doing it. The agent can explain lab results, book appointments, manage meds, all the stuff we thought technology would handle years ago. (If you've been to a doctor lately, you know they're still using clipboards and pens like it's 1995.)

But here's what I love about Amazon's approach: they spent more time in their announcement talking about HIPAA compliance and safety guardrails than flashy features. They're being intentionally cautious, and that's not corporate speak—that's smart.

Healthcare is the one place where people still care deeply about accuracy. This isn't "which AirPods should I buy?" territory. This is "am I dying?" territory. People will forgive AI for a lot of things, but they won't forgive AI for killing them.

Amazon's doing what good AI companies do: they're embedding agents into existing systems with humans in the loop. When the AI doesn't know something, it admits it and hands you off to a real doctor. It's basically smart triage, not medical replacement.

## The Coordination Economy

Here's a story that hit close to home: there's a startup called Phoebe trying to fix home care with AI agents. If you live in the Coachella Valley, you know about this challenge—lots of folks in the sandwich generation, taking care of aging parents while raising kids.

Home care is a $160 billion industry that still runs like a cottage operation. People coordinate shifts on paper, families don't know what's happening with their loved ones, and good luck getting consistent communication.

But here's the key insight: Phoebe isn't replacing home care workers with robots. They're using AI to coordinate the humans better. The agents handle scheduling, communicate with families, track care plans, and make sure everyone knows what's happening when.

This is the pattern I keep seeing in successful AI implementations. It's not about replacement—it's about coordination. AI handling the administrative mess so humans can focus on the actual care.
