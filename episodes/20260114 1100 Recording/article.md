The alarming trend of billionaires building bunkers while simultaneously bemoaning the lack of AI education in schools points to a deeper societal problem. Are the wealthy elite simply preparing for doomsday, or are they hedging their bets against a failing education system that's unable to equip the next generation with the skills needed to thrive in an AI-driven world? This growing divide is not just about access to technology, it's about access to a mindset that encourages experimentation, innovation, and the confidence to build the future.

While open-source AI is democratizing access to tools like Claude and Chat GPT, a two-tiered future is emerging where some children grow up in environments that foster a building mindset, while others are taught to follow rules and fear failure. The solution lies in visionary leadership, capital, and permission to build a parallel track for AI education. This means starting small with pilot programs and partnerships with universities to demonstrate the value of AI-native education. It also means addressing the structural issues that prevent schools from adapting quickly, such as bureaucratic hurdles and resistance from unions and parents.

Ultimately, the goal is not to overthrow the education system, but to build a proof of concept that proves the old system is incomplete. By building the future in the margins, we can create a compelling alternative that encourages people to choose it. This requires a shift in mindset, from fearing AI to embracing it as an opportunity to transform education and create a more equitable future for all students. The first step? Provide the permission to experiment and build, so that students and educators alike can learn to thrive in an AI-driven world.
