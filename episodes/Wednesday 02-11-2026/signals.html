Computer Science Enrollment Drops Amid AI Job Displacement Fears

Context

For the first time since the dot-com bust in the early 2000s, undergraduate computer science enrollment across the University of California system has declined, reflecting growing uncertainty about traditional tech career paths as AI reshapes the software development industry. Only UC San Diego defied this downward trend, likely because it was the only UC campus to launch an undergraduate artificial intelligence major last year. The National Student Clearinghouse Research Center reported that computer science enrollment fell by almost 8% nationally, with graduate enrollment dropping twice as fast. This decline coincides with unprecedented job losses in the technology sector, where 127,000 workers in US-based tech companies were laid off in 2025, and big tech companies reduced new graduate hiring by 25%. College counselors report hearing fears from both students and parents about whether computer science degrees will lead to stable employment anymore, with many CS graduates describing job searches taking six months to a year, submitting hundreds of applications while competing against experienced developers who were laid off.
